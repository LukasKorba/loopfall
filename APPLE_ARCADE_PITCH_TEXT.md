# LOOPFALL — Apple Arcade Pitch

LOOPFALL began as a prototype in 2014 — a ball on a torus, two taps, pure physics. For over a decade it lived in my head as the game I kept coming back to, refining, reimagining. This year I finally shipped it the way I always envisioned: a hypnotic neon tunnel runner that's simple enough to play in ten seconds, deep enough to chase leaderboards for months — and now big enough to carry a second, completely distinct mode on the same torus.

The year before that prototype, I had shipped **Octagon** — a minimalist hardcore runner with seventy parametric obstacle types that found its audience among players who love games where one mistake equals instant death. Octagon taught me what that audience actually wants: punishing but fair, instantly readable, respectful of the player's time. Loopfall grew directly out of it — Octagon's unforgiving DNA combined with the one-tap impulse mechanic of Flappy Bird, and my own taste for the kind of hardcore game I want to play myself. Where Octagon was flat and sharp, Loopfall is curved and luminous — but the refusal to hold the player's hand is the same.

## The Game

Nudge a ball left or right through gates as the track spirals endlessly inside a glowing torus. The physics-based controls mean every input carries real weight — timing, rhythm, and anticipation emerge naturally as you learn to read the curvature. Death is instant. Restarts are seamless. The track never plays the same run twice.

The signature CMYK chromatic-aberration aesthetic — flat geometry split into three color channels against deep blacks — gives LOOPFALL an identity that reads instantly in a screenshot and holds up beautifully on an OLED display.

## Two Modes, One Torus

**Gates to Hell** is the original: an endless-runner meditation on rhythm and precision. Pass gates, earn swings, chase streaks. Pure flow state.

**Path to Redemption** is the game reborn as arcade combat. Your ball auto-fires laser beams forward; swings let you aim. Destroy sentinels, collect orbs, upgrade your cannon and cadence, deploy shields. Voice callouts escalate as you max out upgrades. It's Space Invaders on a torus — frenzied, loud, completely different in feel from the meditative core mode — but built on the same physics, shaders, and controls.

Both modes are available from the first launch — no grinding, no unlocks, no gatekeeping. One game, two experiences, zero friction.

## What Ships on Day One

- Physics-driven controls with real momentum — touch, keyboard, and MFi controller support
- First-time tutorial — one-shot pass on the core nudge mechanic; mode-specific systems are discovered by play, not explained
- 23 achievements and Game Center leaderboards across both modes, with iCloud sync for stats and best scores across iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple TV
- **15 languages shipped**: English, German, Spanish, French, Italian, Russian, Brazilian Portuguese, Dutch, Polish, Turkish, Ukrainian, Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, Japanese, Korean
- Original soundtrack (10 tracks), full SFX suite, dozens of voice lines
- iOS · iPadOS · tvOS · macOS universal, 60fps on every supported device

## Arcade-Ready

LOOPFALL has **no in-app purchases, no ads, no tracking, no third-party analytics**. Premium feel from first launch. The monetization model fits Apple Arcade cleanly because it was never built for anything else.

## What's Next

LOOPFALL is architected as a platform for ongoing content:

- Cosmetic skins — balls, trails, color themes (code scaffolding in place)
- Seasonal challenges — daily and weekly leaderboard events
- Boss gates, branching paths, moving obstacles
